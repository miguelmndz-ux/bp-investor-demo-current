## Where Successful Launches Happen

A modern product launch is not an event — it's an orchestrated multi-channel arc spanning 60–90 days, with a peak that lasts just 24–48 hours.

### Primary Channels

**Product Hunt**
The #1 destination for tech product launches. 50–120 hours of prep for a top-performing launch. Best for early adopter acquisition, SEO backlinks, press exposure, and investor credibility. 500–5,000 signups typical for top-5 finishes.
- Launch timing: midnight PST for best coverage
- Hunter relationships drive 30%+ of upvotes
- Gallery, video, and tagline quality are decisive
- Community engagement on launch day matters

**Social & Communities**
Twitter/X, LinkedIn, Reddit (r/SaaS, r/startups, r/entrepreneur), Hacker News, and niche Discord/Slack communities drive substantial launch day traffic. Warm outreach to personal networks is the single highest-conversion channel.
- Personal newsletter > cold outreach, always
- Twitter timing and thread structure matter
- Reddit requires authentic engagement, not spam
- Founders' own communities outperform paid

**Press & Media**
TechCrunch, VentureBeat, The Verge for B2C; niche trade press and newsletters for B2B. Press rarely responds to cold pitches — relationships and timing matter enormously.
- Embargoed releases with top 3 outlets max
- Niche newsletters (Every.to, Lenny's) outperform Mashable
- Data stories + founder angle get coverage
- Timing: 8–10AM ET weekday press outreach

### Secondary / Underused Channels

**Alternative Launch Platforms**
Betalist, Microlaunch, Fazier, Hacker News Show HN, Indie Hackers, AppSumo (for LTD deals).
- Betalist — pre-launch waitlist generation
- Show HN — developer-focused, very high signal
- AppSumo — revenue-focused, lifetime deal model
- Microlaunch — PH alternative, growing fast

**Email & SDR Outreach** *(Underused)*
The most underused launch channel for founders. A pre-built email list of relevant prospects outperforms viral social every time for B2B.
- Waitlists built 30–60 days before are gold
- Launch day = email unlock moment
- B2B: 50–300 signups from list = great launch
- Follow-up sequences are almost never built

**Post-Launch Platforms** *(Underused)*
G2, Capterra, Crunchbase, LinkedIn, and SEO are the long-tail of launch momentum. Most founders stop at day 2.
- G2/Capterra reviews: launch day ask from early users
- PH relaunch possible after 6 months
- SEO content from launch assets has 12-month value
- LinkedIn organic reach is underexploited by founders

What separates a $50K ARR launch from a $500K ARR launch is rarely the product — it's the persistence and planning of the outreach beyond day one.

| Period | Key Activities |

## What Defines a Successful Launch

Success is not a rank on a leaderboard. A launch strategy should be judged across four time horizons.

### Four Time Horizons

**24 Hours — Launch Day KPIs**
- Unique visitors vs. baseline
- Signups / trial activations
- PH rank (top 5 = meaningful)
- Press mentions / social shares
- Revenue (B2B: pipeline opened)

**7 Days — Week-One Signals**
- Activation rate of new signups
- Retention day 3 and day 7
- User feedback themes (qualitative)
- Inbound requests (press, BD, investors)
- NPS from launch cohort

**30 Days — Month-One Metrics**
- Trial → paid conversion rate
- CAC from launch cohort
- LTV trajectory signals
- Category share of voice
- Backlinks & SEO impact

**90 Days — Quarter Outcome**
- Revenue run rate established
- Churn rate of launch cohort
- Category leader position
- Team expansion enabled
- Follow-on raise potential

### OKR Framework for Launch Success

**Objectives**
- Establish competitive market position and category awareness
- Drive initial customer acquisition and product validation
- Build brand credibility with press, investors, and community
- Activate revenue engine (trial → paid funnel)

**Key Results**
- #1–5 on Product Hunt on launch day
- 500+ signups in first 72 hours (B2C: 3,000+)
- 5x+ traffic spike vs. 30-day baseline
- 2+ press placements in target outlets
- >40% activation rate from launch cohort

## Our Concept: Apex — The BuildParty Product Launch Agent

Not a checklist. Not a copy generator. A persistent, multi-phase AI agent that becomes the founder's launch director — planning, executing, monitoring, and compounding the momentum of every product launch.

### Three Modes of Engagement

**Mode 1 — AI-Assisted (Human + Agent Collaboration)**
The agent generates strategy, assets, timelines, and recommendations. The founder reviews and approves at each gate. Works like having a senior launch consultant available 24/7 at 1% of the cost.

**Mode 2 — Human-in-the-Loop (Agent-Led with Human Gates)**
The agent executes autonomously — scheduling posts, sending outreach, engaging the PH community, monitoring upvotes, triggering follow-up sequences — with human approval at key moments. The sweet spot for most founders.

**Mode 3 — Fully Agentic (Autonomous Launch Execution)**
For advanced users or agencies managing multiple launches simultaneously. The agent runs the full playbook with real-time dashboard visibility and exception-only human touchpoints. The future state.
