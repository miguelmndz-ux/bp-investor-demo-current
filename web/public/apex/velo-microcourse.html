Module 0
Velo Mental Model & Orientationcheck
20–30 min
Module Outcome
Understand what Velo is, how its AI pipeline works, and which plan you need before touching the app.

Before you record your first video, it helps to understand what Velo actually does under the hood. Velo is not a screen recorder like Loom or Zoom. It is an AI video production tool — you give it raw input (a recording, slides, or a URL), and it returns a polished video with a script, an AI voice, and an AI avatar.

What Velo Is (and Is Not)

Velo IS

An AI pipeline that takes raw input and produces a script + voice + avatar composited video. Great for product demos, onboarding, and sales content.

Velo is NOT

A basic screen recorder (not Loom), a video editor (not Camtasia), or an audio enhancer. It rewrites the narrative from scratch.

The Full AI Pipeline

Every Velo video — no matter which input mode you use — travels through the same five-step pipeline:

  1. Raw Input — You provide a screen recording, uploaded file, slides, or a URL.
  2. Script Generation — Velo's AI analyzes what it sees (cursor movement, UI elements, timing) and writes a narration script.
  3. Voice Cloning — The script is read aloud using your VeloTwin voice (or a public voice if you don't have one yet).
  4. Avatar Compositing — Your AI face is overlaid on the video, lip-synced to the voice.
  5. Share Link — Velo publishes the finished video and gives you a shareable URL — no download required.

The 4 Input Modes at a Glance

Screen Capture

Record your screen live using the Chrome extension. Best for live product walkthroughs with any login-gated flow.

Upload a Recording

Upload an existing MP4, MOV, or WEBM (from Zoom, Gong, etc.). Velo writes a brand-new script from your footage.

Upload PDFs & PNGs

Slide decks, mockups, feature screenshots. Velo navigates the slides and generates per-slide narration. Currently Beta.

Paste a Link

Give Velo a public URL. Its AI browser agent navigates the product autonomously and records a demo. Pro/Ultra only.

Requirements Before You Start

  • Browser: Chrome only — Safari, Firefox, and Edge are not supported.
  • Extension: Velo Companion must be installed and pinned from the Chrome Web Store.
  • Microphone: Required for voice cloning and live screen capture. Test it before your first recording.
  • Device: Desktop only — Velo does not support mobile recording.

Module 1
Your First Velo: Screen Capture Modecheck
45–60 min
Module Outcome
Record, process, and publish a complete Velo video end-to-end using Screen Capture — all in one sitting.

Screen Capture is Velo's most direct input mode: you share your screen, narrate what you're doing, and Velo handles everything else. This module walks you through every step — from installing the Chrome extension to seeing your finished share link in the browser.

Step 1: Install & Pin the Velo Companion Extension

  1. Open Chrome and go to the Chrome Web Store. Search "Velo Companion".
  2. Click Add to Chrome. When prompted, click Add extension.
  3. In your Chrome toolbar, click the puzzle piece icon (Extensions menu). Find Velo Companion and click the pin icon so it always shows in your toolbar.
  4. Confirm the Velo icon appears in your toolbar. If it doesn't, the extension didn't install — try again in a fresh Chrome window.

Step 2: Set Up Your Recording

Click the Velo Companion icon to open the recording launcher. Before you hit Record, configure three things:

Window / Tab Selection

Choose Tab to capture a single browser tab (recommended — cleaner background). Use Window only if your demo spans multiple apps.

Resolution

Record at your screen's native resolution. Don't zoom in your browser before recording — Velo handles visual zoom in the editor afterward.

Microphone Check

Confirm the correct mic is selected. Speak a test phrase. If the level bar doesn't move, switch input devices before proceeding.

Step 3: Record Well

Your narration and cursor movement directly shape the AI script Velo generates. Use this technique:

  • Slow your clicks. Pause 1–2 seconds after each click so Velo can recognize the action before you move on.
  • Narrate naturally. Speak like you're showing a colleague — avoid scripting or over-enunciating. The AI rewrites it anyway.
  • Pause before transitions. When moving between sections of the product, take a breath. This gives Velo clean segment breaks.
  • Filler words are fine. "Um", "uh", and "so" are stripped by the AI. Long mid-sentence pauses are more disruptive than filler words.

Step 4: What Happens After You Stop Recording

When you click Stop, Velo's AI pipeline starts immediately. Do not close the tab. Here's what's happening:

  1. Velo analyzes your recording — cursor position, click events, timing, and UI content visible on screen.
  2. A script is generated: one line per segment, one segment per distinct action.
  3. The script is passed to the voice engine, which reads it using your selected VeloTwin voice.
  4. The avatar is composited over the video — face, lip sync, and eye movement all happen at this stage.
  5. You're placed into the Velo editor with the finished draft ready to review.
Processing time: A 3-minute recording typically takes 3–8 minutes to process, depending on server load. Don't refresh the page — processing continues in the background even if the progress indicator looks stuck.

Step 5: Your First Look at the Editor

In this module, accept the auto-generated script as-is. Don't edit yet — script editing is covered in Module 5. For now, just observe:

  • The Scripts panel (right sidebar) shows the narration text broken into segments. Each line corresponds to one video chunk.
  • The timeline at the bottom shows your video laid out over time. Each segment appears as a block.
  • The Face panel (right sidebar) lets you choose or switch your VeloTwin voice and avatar.

Step 6: Choose a Public VeloTwin & Publish

If you don't have a custom VeloTwin yet, open the Face panel and choose one of Velo's public avatars. Pick one whose tone matches your content — professional and neutral is a safe default for a first video.

Once you've selected a voice, click Publish. Velo finalizes the render and generates your share link. Copy it and open it in a new tab to see exactly what your recipients will see.

Common Failure Points

Connection Drops

If your internet disconnects mid-recording, the upload fails silently. Record on a stable connection and keep the browser tab active throughout.

Blocked Permissions

If Chrome doesn't ask to use your mic, permissions are blocked. Go to Chrome Settings → Privacy → Microphone and allow velo.video.

Full Desktop vs. Tab

Sharing full desktop includes your taskbar, notifications, and other apps. Always share a specific tab unless you genuinely need multi-app coverage in your demo.

Module 2
All Four Input Modescheck
1.5–2 hrs
Module Outcome
Confidently execute all 4 input modes and choose the right one for any production scenario.

You used Screen Capture in Module 1. Now you'll learn the other three modes — when to use each, how each feeds into the same AI pipeline, and how to pick the right mode every time without defaulting to Screen Capture out of habit.

Mode 2: Upload a Recording

You already have footage — a Zoom call, a Gong session, a QuickTime recording, an OBS capture. Instead of re-recording, upload it directly to Velo.

Accepted Formats

MP4, MOV, WEBM. Maximum file size: 1 GB. Convert other formats using QuickTime (Mac) or HandBrake (Windows) before uploading.

What Velo Does With It

Velo generates a brand-new script from your footage visuals. It does not clean up your original audio or enhance your narration — it replaces it entirely.

Best Sources

Zoom call recordings, Gong session clips, Fathom highlights, QuickTime screen recordings, OBS captures.

Key insight: Upload mode is ideal for repurposing existing recordings. If your original video has good screen content but rambling narration, Upload mode is the right call — Velo discards the narration and writes a clean script from what it sees on screen.

Mode 3: Upload PDFs & PNGs (Beta)

Have a slide deck, feature mockup, or unreleased UI you want to narrate? Upload it as a PDF or image file — no live product needed.

  • Accepted formats: PDF, PNG, JPG, GIF, WebP
  • Limits: Up to 10 files, 100 MB each
  • How it works: Velo treats each slide or image as a scene. It navigates through them in order and generates per-slide narration automatically.
  • Requires: Velo Companion Chrome Extension (currently Beta)
  • Bonus option: For slide decks, Velo also offers an agentic browser recording — it opens your slides in a browser and records itself presenting them.

Mode 4: Paste a Link (Agentic Mode)

This is the most powerful — and most temperamental — input mode. Give Velo a URL and a plain-English description of the flow you want, and Velo's AI browser agent navigates the product on its own and records a demo.

How It Works

You write a plain-English description of the flow you want demonstrated. Velo's agent opens a real browser, navigates the site autonomously, and records what it does.

Writing Good Instructions

Be specific. Bad: "show the app." Good: "Navigate to the Pricing page, click Compare Plans, highlight the Pro column, then click Start Free Trial."

Limitations

Cannot access Cloudflare-protected sites, login-gated pages, or pages behind authentication walls. Beta status means occasional failures — have a Screen Capture fallback ready.

Usage Limits

Pro 5 uses/month   Ultra Unlimited   Free Not available

When Agentic fails, don't retry blindly. If Velo's agent gets stuck or produces a broken demo, switch to Screen Capture instead. Agentic mode is powerful for public URLs but unreliable for anything requiring a login or Cloudflare challenge.

Use this table every time you start a new video. Pick the mode that matches your situation.

Pitch deck, QBR slides, feature mockup, unreleased UI PDF / PNG No live product needed. Works directly from design assets or slides.

Module 3
The Editor: Every Panel, Timeline & Toolbarcheck
1–1.5 hrs
Module Outcome
Navigate and operate every control surface in the Velo editor with confidence — right sidebar panels, timeline tracks, and toolbar tools.

After Velo processes your recording, you land in the editor. It has three main areas: the right sidebar (8 panels for video settings), the timeline at the bottom (6 track layers), and the toolbar above the timeline (editing tools). This module covers all of them.

Right Sidebar — 8 Panels

The right sidebar is where you configure everything about how your video looks and sounds. Click each panel name to open it.

Screen / Background

Set your background style: Wallpaper (preset images), Gradient, or Solid color. Adjust the blur slider to soften the background behind your recording. Use the padding slider to add breathing room around your screen content. Set aspect ratio: 16:9, 9:16, 1:1, or 4:3.

Scripts

View your auto-generated transcript. Click the lock icon to unlock editing. You can edit up to +20% more words than the original script. After editing, click Re-render to update the voice — editing text alone doesn't change the audio.

Insert

Add visual elements: Text boxes, images, shapes, arrows. Add effects: Callout boxes (highlight a UI element) and Spotlight (dim surrounding areas). Add focus tools: Mask (hide sensitive content) and Zoom (draw attention to an area).

Cards

Control your intro card — the title screen that appears at the start. Toggle it on/off, choose light or dark mode, set your Name/Role, Title (30-character limit), and Description (200-character limit).

Face

Toggle your avatar on/off. Switch between VeloTwins. Use the 9-point position grid to place the avatar (corners, edges, or center). Adjust size slider. Choose avatar shape: original, square, or round bubble. Enable zoom shrink behavior so the avatar shrinks when zoom blocks are active.

Audio

Browse and add background music from Velo's library. Adjust the master volume and control narration volume independently from music volume. Best practice: keep music at 20–30% of narration level.

Settings

Set your video's title, description, and upload a logo for the share page. This metadata appears when someone opens your share link.

Cursor

Toggle cursor visibility. Choose cursor style: classic, hand, or high-contrast. Adjust size. Enable hide-when-still with a delay. Turn on Smart Rotate for a natural feel. Add click effects: Circle or Ripple (with adjustable volume for click sounds).

Timeline — 6 Track Layers

The timeline runs along the bottom of the editor. It shows your video broken into tracks stacked on top of each other. Think of each track as a separate layer of your video.

Camera Track

Shows your VeloTwin face segments. You can toggle the avatar on or off for individual segments — useful for sections where the avatar would block important UI.

Video Track

The primary track — your original screen recording. This is the base layer everything else sits on top of. You can't remove it, but you can cut and crop it.

Zoom Track

Zoom blocks live here. Each block zooms into a specific area of your screen for its duration. Use Auto mode for cursor-following zoom, or Manual mode to lock the zoom target. Choose Instant or Smooth transition between zoom levels.

Masking Track

Mask blocks obscure a region of your screen for their duration. Place them over any email addresses, passwords, private dashboard numbers, or PII visible in your recording. The masked area appears as a blurred or solid block.

Spotlight Track

Spotlight blocks dim the area around the spotlight, drawing the viewer's eye to a specific UI element. Adjust border radius (circular vs. rectangular) and opacity (60–80% is usually readable without being too dark).

Callout Track

Callout blocks add a labeled highlight box over a specific UI element. Useful for labeling buttons or fields the viewer needs to find on their own later. Adjust scale, opacity, color, and border radius. Keep duration short: 2–4 seconds is ideal.

Toolbar

The toolbar sits above the timeline and gives you editing controls:

  • Undo / Redo — Step backward or forward through your edit history.
  • Playback controls — Play, pause, and scrub through the timeline to preview your video.
  • Cut tool (✂) — Splits the video at the playhead position. Use this to remove sections or create clean segment breaks.
  • Crop tool (⊡) — Trim the start or end of your video using handles. Drag inward to shorten, drag outward to restore.
  • Timeline zoom slider — Zoom in on the timeline for precise block placement. Zoom out to see the full video at once.
  • Fullscreen preview — Preview your video at full size to check how it will look on the share page.
Key Terms
Zoom Track ▾
A timeline layer that contains zoom blocks. Each zoom block magnifies a region of your screen during its duration. Auto mode follows the cursor; Manual mode locks to a fixed target area. You can choose instant or smooth transitions between zoom levels.
Masking Track ▾
A timeline layer for placing mask blocks that obscure regions of your screen. Use it to hide PII (personally identifiable information), passwords, email addresses, or private dashboard data before sharing a video. Masks are time-based — they apply only during the block's duration on the timeline.
Spotlight Track ▾
A timeline layer for spotlight blocks that dim everything around a focused region. The effect draws viewer attention to a specific UI element without hiding the rest of the screen. Opacity of 60–80% is typically the right balance between readable and focused.
Callout Track ▾

Module 4
VeloTwin: Create, Calibrate & Polish Your AI Avatarcheck
1–1.5 hrs
Module Outcome
Create a photorealistic, well-calibrated custom VeloTwin that produces natural, professional output across every video.

Your VeloTwin is your AI avatar — the face and voice that appear in every Velo video. Getting it right is a one-time investment that pays off on every video you publish afterward. This module walks through the full setup: face capture, voice cloning, and in-editor calibration.

Public VeloTwins vs. Custom VeloTwins

Public VeloTwins

Pre-built avatars from Velo's library. Available on Free and Pro plans. No setup required. Good for testing the platform before committing to a custom avatar. You share the face with other Velo users.

Custom VeloTwin

Your own face + your own voice, permanently tied to your account. Ultra only. One-time setup. Produces a professional, recognizable presence across all your videos. This is what makes Velo compelling for a personal brand.

Not on Ultra yet? Complete this module with a public avatar. The calibration steps (positioning, size, shape, zoom shrink) apply to public VeloTwins too. When you upgrade, just re-run the face capture and voice cloning steps.

Face Capture (One-Time Setup)

Velo's face capture takes a series of photos to build your animated AI face. Before you start the countdown flow inside Velo, set up your environment correctly:

  • Lighting: Even, front-facing light — no harsh shadows, no backlit windows. A ring light or a lamp placed in front of you both work well.
  • Background: Neutral, solid color. Avoid white (blows out in the composite) and avoid anything patterned or busy.
  • Camera position: Eye level. Your face should fill the frame without being cropped. Center yourself.

Follow Velo's countdown photo flow inside the app. What Velo generates from your photos: natural blinks, head movement, eye gestures, and lip sync matched to your voice clone.

Voice Cloning

Velo clones your voice from a training script you read aloud. The quality of your clone depends almost entirely on how you record it:

  1. Read naturally. Use your normal speaking voice, at your normal pace. Do not over-enunciate — the clone captures your natural cadence, and exaggerated speech produces an unnatural clone.
  2. Use a quiet room. Background noise (AC hum, traffic, keyboard clicks) gets baked into the clone and becomes audible in every video.
  3. Maintain consistent distance. Keep the same distance from your mic for the entire training session. Distance changes produce inconsistent audio levels in the clone.
  4. Read the full script. Don't skip sentences. Velo uses every sample to build the model — partial readings produce a weaker clone.
Tonality matters. The voice clone captures your accent, cadence, and vocal character. If you're having a flat, monotone day, reschedule the recording. Your clone will reflect exactly the energy you bring to the training session.

In-Editor Calibration (Face Panel)

After your VeloTwin is created, you calibrate its appearance in every video using the Face panel in the editor:

Positioning (9-Point Grid)

Use the rule of thirds: place the avatar in a corner or edge, not the center. The avatar should frame your screen content — not compete with it. Bottom corners work well for most product demos.

Size

Large enough to read as a human face (viewer should be able to see your expressions), but small enough not to block key UI elements. A good starting point: 15–20% of the video width.

Shape

Choose round bubble for a casual, friendly feel (great for onboarding and internal videos). Choose rectangular for a more formal, documentary-style look (better for executive briefings and sales demos).

Zoom Shrink

Enable this if you're using zoom blocks frequently. When a zoom block is active, the avatar automatically shrinks out of the way so it doesn't block the zoomed-in area you're highlighting.

Avatar Length Limits

Plan Max Avatar Video Length Notes
Pro 3 minutes Sufficient for most product demos and feature updates
Ultra 30 minutes Required for long-form onboarding, training, or full product tours

When to Re-Record

Re-take the Face Capture If…

The lighting was uneven (one side of your face darker), shadows cut across your face, your background was cluttered, or the initial capture looks flat or unnatural in the editor.

Re-record the Voice If…

The clone sounds monotone compared to your normal speech, it struggles with your name or company terms, the background noise is audible in the clone output, or you've changed your speaking style significantly.

Module 5
Script Mastery: Generation, Editing & Promptingcheck
1–1.5 hrs
Module Outcome
Produce predictable, on-tone scripts by mastering manual editing, context prompting, retries, and voice regeneration.

The script is the core of every Velo video. Everything else — voice, avatar, timeline effects — is presentation. If the script is wrong, the video is wrong. This module gives you four tools to control script quality: editing, context prompting, retries, and voice regeneration.

How Velo Generates Scripts

Understanding how Velo writes your first draft helps you predict where it'll go wrong — and fix it faster.

What It Analyzes

Velo reads your cursor position, click events, screen content, and timing. It uses visual cues — what's on screen, what you clicked, when you paused — not your spoken narration.

Script Structure

One line = one segment = one video chunk. Each line in the Scripts panel corresponds to one block on the timeline. Editing a line changes that chunk's narration.

Where First Drafts Fall Short

Typical issues: too much technical jargon, wrong emphasis (describing what you clicked instead of why), and occasional factual errors about what the UI actually does.

Expect 80% accuracy on first draft. Velo's AI is good at describing what it sees, but it doesn't know your audience or your message. Manual tuning and context prompting close the gap.

Manual Script Editing

  1. Open the Scripts panel in the right sidebar. Click the lock icon to unlock editing.
  2. Edit the text directly. You can change, remove, or rewrite any line — but stay within the +20% word count limit. Adding more than 20% over the original word count per segment is not allowed.
  3. After editing, click Re-render audio. Editing the text alone does not update the voice — you must explicitly trigger a re-render for changes to take effect in the audio.

What to edit: Remove filler phrases ("so basically", "what we're going to do here is"). Fix jargon that your audience won't recognize. Correct wrong emphasis. Adjust pacing — shorter sentences speak more naturally than long ones.

Context Prompting — Rewrite with a Tone

Instead of editing line by line, you can give Velo a context prompt — a description of your audience, tone, and goal — and have it rewrite the entire script at once. This is faster than manual editing for tone corrections.

Use these three prompt templates as your starting point. Copy each one and customize it for your video:

Executive audience — skip technical steps, focus on business outcomes and time-to-value
Sales demo tone — benefit-led opening, focus on pain point solved, close with a clear next step
Tutorial tone — instructional, step-by-step, patient pace, name every UI element the viewer needs to find
Build a prompt library. Save your 3 most-used context prompts in a doc (see Module 7 for the full playbook approach). The goal: never write a new context prompt from scratch for a video type you've done before.

Script Retries

A script retry asks Velo to generate a completely fresh script from your original recording — different word choice, emphasis, and structure. Use retries when the first draft is fundamentally off, not when you just need to tweak a few lines.

When to Retry

The script missed the point entirely. The structure is wrong. The tone is completely off. A retry has a chance of getting the structure right from scratch.

When to Edit Manually

The script is basically right but specific lines need fixing. Manual editing is faster and targeted — don't burn a retry on a 2-line problem.

Retry Limits

Pro 3 retries per video   Ultra 10 retries per video. Use retries strategically — don't run through them speculatively.

Voice Regenerations

After editing the script text, you must re-render the voice. This is called a voice regeneration. It is separate from a script retry:

  • Script retry: Velo rewrites the script from scratch. Costs one retry. Does NOT cost a voice regeneration.
  • Voice regeneration: Velo re-reads the current script with the voice model. Required after any manual text edit. Costs one regeneration.
  • Limits: Pro 3 regenerations/video   Ultra 10 regenerations/video
Don't burn regenerations on minor edits. Make all your text changes at once, then regenerate once. Regenerating after each individual line edit wastes your budget.
Key Terms
Script Retry ▾
A complete script regeneration from your original recording. Velo generates a brand-new script — different word choice, structure, and emphasis. Pro: 3 per video. Ultra: 10 per video. Use when the first draft is fundamentally wrong, not for minor tweaks.
Context Prompt ▾
A plain-English description of your audience, tone, and goal that you give to Velo before it generates (or regenerates) a script. Example: "Executive audience — skip technical steps, focus on business outcomes." Context prompts steer the tone and structure of the entire script at once.
Voice Regeneration ▾
The process of re-recording the script audio with the voice model after you've edited the script text. Required whenever you change script text — Velo does not automatically update the audio when you type. Pro: 3 per video. Ultra: 10 per video.
Word Count Limit ▾
The constraint on manual script editing: you can add up to +20% more words than the original segment's word count. Going over this limit is not allowed. If you need to say significantly more than the original script, consider splitting the segment or using a context prompt to restructure.
Script Segment ▾
A single chunk of video content — one line in the Scripts panel, one block on the timeline. Each segment has its own narration text and corresponding video clip. Editing one segment's text only changes that chunk's audio.

Production Quality: Brand Kit & Strategic Timeline Layerscheck
1.5–2 hrs
Module Outcome
Produce videos that look consistent, professional, and brand-aligned using brand kit, intro cards, and strategic annotation tools.

A technically correct video that looks inconsistent or unpolished undermines your credibility. This module covers the visual and audio production layer: brand kit setup, intro cards, background decisions, cursor customization, and strategic use of the six timeline annotation tools.

Brand Kit Setup

Your brand kit is the visual identity that appears across every video. Set it up once and apply it as your default so you never start from zero.

  • Logo: Upload your company or personal logo. It appears on the share page when anyone opens your share link.
  • Brand colors: Set your primary and secondary colors. These are applied to backgrounds, intro cards, and callout styling throughout the editor.
  • Personas: A persona bundles a brand kit configuration — colors, logo, avatar, and default settings — under a named profile. Create one persona per brand, product line, or client if you're producing videos for multiple contexts.
  • Set as default: Make your configured brand kit the default for all new videos so you never have to reapply it manually.

Intro Cards

An intro card is a title screen that plays at the start of your video before the screen recording begins. It sets context and adds immediate professionalism.

Light vs. Dark Mode

Match your brand. Dark mode feels premium and modern. Light mode feels cleaner and more approachable. Choose one and use it consistently across your video library.

Copywriting the Card

Name/Role: Your name and title. Title: 30-character limit — make it the video's core topic, not your name. Description: 200-character limit — one sentence on what the viewer will learn or see.

When to Skip It

Intro cards work best for external videos (prospects, stakeholders). For quick internal screen recordings or informal team updates, they can add unnecessary formality. Use judgment.

Background & Format Decisions

Setting Options Best For
Background style Wallpaper, Gradient, Solid color Wallpaper for brand storytelling; Gradient for modern demos; Solid for minimal distraction
Blur slider 0–100% Creates visual depth behind the recording. 60–80% softens the background without losing context.
Padding slider 0–max Adds breathing room around the screen content. More padding = smaller recording within the frame.
Aspect ratio 16:9, 9:16, 1:1, 4:3 16:9 for desktop and email; 9:16 for LinkedIn and mobile; 1:1 for social posts; 4:3 for legacy contexts

Cursor Customization

  • Cursor style: Classic (standard arrow), Hand (pointing finger), or High-contrast (for viewers with accessibility needs or small screens).
  • Smart Rotate: Rotates the cursor to follow movement direction. Adds a natural, fluid feel — enable for most product demos.
  • Click effects: Circle (subtle, professional) or Ripple (more visible, can distract). Use click effects sparingly — one effect throughout, not mixed.
  • Hide when still: Hides the cursor after a set delay when you're not moving. Reduces visual clutter during static moments where you're just narrating.

Strategic Timeline Layers — When & How

You learned what each layer is in Module 3. Now you'll learn when to use each one — and which combinations work vs. which ones create noise.

Zoom Blocks

When: Use to draw attention to a specific UI element during a key step. Auto mode for cursor-following demos — the zoom tracks wherever you click. Manual mode to lock focus on a specific area regardless of cursor movement.

Spotlight

When: Use for complex UIs with a lot of noise around the element you're demonstrating. Sets opacity carefully — 60–80% dims enough to focus attention without making the rest of the screen unreadable.

Callout Boxes

When: Use to label UI elements the viewer will need to find on their own after watching. Duration: 2–4 seconds — long enough to register, short enough not to linger. Don't use callouts on things the camera is already on.

Masks

Always: Mask any PII, passwords, email addresses, private dashboard numbers, or internal data before publishing. Place mask blocks on the timeline (not as static overlays) so they cover the sensitive data only during the frames where it appears.

Layering strategy: Zoom + Callout together = maximum clarity on a key step (zoom in, then label the element). Avoid Zoom + Spotlight together — two focus effects compete and create visual noise.
Always mask PII before publishing. Any video with an email address, password, or private dashboard data visible in the recording must have those elements masked before you share the link. Review your video frame by frame for sensitive content before hitting Publish.

Audio Balance

  • Background music: Use sparingly for product demos — music can undermine the professional feel. Reserve it for showcase or announcement videos.
  • Narration vs. music volume: Narration should dominate completely. Set music to 20–30% of the narration level — it should be ambient, not noticeable.
  • Test on headphones: Background music that sounds subtle in your speakers can be loud and distracting on headphones. Always check before publishing.
Key Terms
Brand Kit ▾
A reusable visual identity configuration in Velo: your logo, brand colors, default avatar, and persona settings. Set it as your default so every new video starts with consistent branding applied automatically.
Persona ▾
A named bundle of brand kit settings — colors, logo, avatar, and defaults — inside Velo. Useful when producing videos for multiple brands, clients, or contexts. Switch personas to instantly apply a different visual identity to a video.
Intro Card ▾
A title screen that plays at the beginning of a Velo video before the screen recording starts. Contains your name/role, a title (30-char max), and a description (200-char max). Adds perceived professionalism to external-facing videos.
Aspect Ratio ▾
The width-to-height ratio of your video frame. 16:9 is standard for desktop and email. 9:16 (vertical) is optimized for LinkedIn and mobile viewing. 1:1 (square) works for social media posts. 4:3 is used in legacy or presentation contexts.
Zoom Shrink ▾
A Face panel setting that shrinks the VeloTwin avatar automatically when a zoom block is active on the timeline, preventing it from blocking the zoomed-in UI area. Covered in detail in Module 4.
PII (Personally Identifiable Information) ▾
Any data that could identify a real person: email addresses, names, phone numbers, account numbers, or private dashboard data. Must always be masked in Velo videos before publishing. Use the Masking track to cover these elements on the timeline.

By now you have all the tools. This module is about systematizing them — turning what you've learned into a repeatable workflow so that the third video is as good as the tenth, and you never waste Velo Minutes on preventable mistakes.

Your Repeatable Production Workflow

Every Velo video should follow the same loop. The goal is to reduce decision fatigue — you decide once, then execute every time.

  1. Pre-production checklist: Flow planned and tested. Talking points ready. Chrome extension pinned. Mic tested. Brand kit set as default.
  2. Record or input: Choose your input mode (use the decision framework below). Execute the recording or upload.
  3. Review script: Read all lines. Identify what's wrong before touching anything.
  4. Apply context prompt: Use your saved prompt template for this video type. Re-render audio after changes.
  5. Set avatar and brand kit: Confirm the right VeloTwin is selected. Confirm brand kit is applied (logo, colors, persona).
  6. Apply timeline effects: Zoom on key clicks, mask any PII, add callouts where needed, enable intro card.
  7. Publish and verify: Generate share link. Open it in a new tab. Confirm it plays correctly and looks exactly as intended.

Time Benchmarks by Video Type

Sales Demo

30–45 minutes total. ~10 min recording, ~20–30 min editing (script + timeline effects). Most of the time goes to script and zoom placement.

Onboarding Video

45–60 minutes total. Longer recording (multiple features), more masking and callouts needed. Budget more time for timeline polish.

Feature Update

20–30 minutes total. Focused scope, usually 1–2 features. Fastest production type when your workflow is established.

Scenario Best Mode
New live product walkthrough, any auth-gated flow Screen Capture
Repurposing a Zoom, Gong, or OBS recording Upload
Pitch deck or slide deck PDF / PNG
Public SaaS product, new browser-accessible flow Paste a Link

  • Brand colors: At least two colors set (primary and secondary).
  • Default VeloTwin: Your custom avatar (or chosen public avatar) set as the default so you never start with an unintended face.
  • Default persona: If you produce videos for multiple contexts, create and name separate personas — one per brand or client.
  • Naming convention: Name your videos consistently: [Product] — [Video Type] — [Date] (e.g., "Acme — Sales Demo — 2026-04").

Pricing Optimization

Free Plan

15 Velo Minutes/month. Good for evaluation only — you'll hit the limit in one or two 5-minute demos. Not suitable for regular production.

Pro Plan ($20/mo)

60 minutes/month. At a 3-minute demo average, that's ~20 demos per month. Budget your 3 retries and 3 voice regenerations per video for important content. Don't use retries speculatively.

Ultra Plan ($200/mo)

600 minutes/month + custom VeloTwin + unlimited agentic + 10 retries. Warranted when you're producing daily, need 30-min demos, or are using Velo across a team.

Watermark strategy: Share links are always watermark-free on all plans — including Free. Only direct downloads from Free are watermarked. When sharing externally, always use share links, never raw file downloads.

Velo Minutes Math

Estimate your monthly minute consumption before you upgrade:

  • Sales demos (avg 3 min each): 3 × [demos/month] = minutes consumed
  • Onboarding videos (avg 5–7 min each): 6 × [videos/month] = minutes consumed
  • Feature updates (avg 2 min each): 2 × [updates/month] = minutes consumed
  • Add a 20% buffer for retries and regenerations that produce slightly longer output
  • If total exceeds 60 min/month consistently, upgrade to Ultra

Video Library System

  • Naming conventions: Use a consistent format: [Context] — [Type] — [YYYY-MM]. Makes searching your library fast.
  • Reuse vs. re-record: Re-share an existing video if the product hasn't changed. Re-record if the UI, flow, or message has changed meaningfully.
  • Regenerate vs. start fresh: Use voice regeneration if only the script needs updating. Start fresh if the recording itself is outdated.
